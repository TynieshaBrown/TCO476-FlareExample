Americus Attractions

The Rylander is a well known historic attraction in Americus. It was originally built in 1921 and operated as a vaudeville theatre. It is located just blocks from the Windsor Hotel, the historic and was restored in the Art Deco style of design in 1999. It can seat 621 and offers live performances, organ concerts, movies and numerous special events throughout the year.

 

"Built in 1892, to attract winter visitors from the north, the Windsor was a 100-room, five story Victorian structure complete with towers, balconies, and a three story open atrium lobby." The Windsor is one of Americus' most prized possessions. It takes up the entire city block, and was the site of several balls and celebrations. The Windsor closed its doors in the early 1970s, but was recently restored and returned the historic structure to its original grandeur. Visitors can now stay in any of 53 period style rooms with all-modern amenities. No rooms in the Windsor are exactly the same.

 

The Global Village & Discovery Center is a 6-acre village displaying different cultures and poverty areas. It is a "unique way to see life-size Habitat houses from countries around the world without leaving beautiful Southwest Georgia." A lot of schools and organizations visit for school field trips, scouts, youth groups, senior group travelers and tour planners.
